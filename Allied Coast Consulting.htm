About Allied Coast

James LaFlamme

Founder, Allied Coast Consulting

I bring over 15 years of experience in fisheries management, conservation, and business development. My work has always focused on supporting communities in building strong, sustainable, and self-directed fisheries programs. I've led multi-species harvest initiatives, designed and managed operational systems, developed training programs, and helped secure funding to bring local priorities to life.

I've also worked closely with communities, scientists, industry, and government to support conservation efforts and develop practical solutions to complex challenges. From restoring critical marine habitats to navigating regulatory frameworks, I've built a career that balances long-term sustainability with economic opportunity.

In addition to my fisheries work, I have started and operated several small businesses. This entrepreneurial background gives me a practical perspective on what it takes to turn vision into action, especially in remote or resource-based contexts.

Through Allied Coast, I provide consulting and training services that help build capacity, improve operations, and unlock new opportunities in fisheries and wildlife management. My goal is to help clients take control of their own path forward with confidence, clarity, and the right support behind them.

Frequently Asked Questions

What services does Allied Coast provide?

Allied Coast provides a comprehensive range of services focused on fisheries management, conservation, and capacity building for First Nations communities. Our core services include:

  • Capacity building and training programs
  • Fisheries management and quota optimization
  • Funding assistance and proposal writing
  • Regulatory compliance support
  • Business development for resource-based enterprises
  • Technology implementation for operational efficiency

Each service is customized to meet the specific needs and priorities of the communities we work with.

How does Allied Coast work with First Nations communities?

Our approach with First Nations communities is centered on partnership and respect. We believe in meeting communities in their own territories, understanding their unique perspectives, and aligning our work with their priorities and values.

As a 50% First Nations-owned consulting firm, we bring both cultural understanding and technical expertise to every project. We focus on building long-term relationships based on trust and mutual respect, working alongside communities rather than simply providing services to them.

Our goal is to support self-determination by helping communities develop the capacity, resources, and tools they need to manage their own fisheries and natural resources effectively and sustainably.

What experience does James LaFlamme have?

James LaFlamme brings over 15 years of diverse experience in fisheries management, conservation, and business development. His background includes:

  • Leading multi-species harvest initiatives
  • Designing and managing operational systems for fisheries
  • Developing specialized training programs

This combination of technical expertise, practical experience, and entrepreneurial background allows James to address both the ecological and economic aspects of sustainable resource management.

What areas does Allied Coast serve?

Allied Coast primarily serves communities along British Columbia's West Coast, with a focus on Vancouver Island and surrounding regions. Our familiarity with the unique ecological, cultural, and economic characteristics of this region allows us to provide highly relevant and effective services.

However, many of our approaches and methodologies can be adapted to other coastal and resource-based communities throughout British Columbia and beyond. We consider each potential project based on our ability to provide excellent service and meaningful results.

How long does a typical consulting project take?

The timeline for our consulting projects varies greatly depending on the nature and scope of the work. Some focused initiatives may be completed in a matter of weeks, while more comprehensive capacity building programs might unfold over several years.

We believe in taking the time needed to do things right, especially when working with communities to build lasting capacity and sustainable systems. Our approach emphasizes quality and durability over quick fixes.

During our initial consultation, we'll discuss your timeline needs and develop a realistic schedule that balances efficiency with thoroughness.

How does the process work when engaging Allied Coast?

Our engagement process typically follows these steps:

  1. Initial Consultation: We meet with you, preferably in your community, to understand your needs, priorities, and vision.
  2. Assessment: We evaluate current situations, opportunities, and challenges related to your fisheries or resource management goals.
  3. Proposal Development: We create a customized proposal outlining recommended approaches, timelines, and investment required.
  4. Collaborative Planning: We work with you to refine the plan and ensure it aligns with your community's values and objectives.
  5. Implementation: We put the plan into action, working alongside your team to build capacity and transfer knowledge throughout the process.
  6. Evaluation and Adjustment: We regularly assess progress and make adjustments as needed to ensure optimal results.
  7. Ongoing Support: We remain available for follow-up support and additional services as your needs evolve.

Throughout this process, we maintain open communication and emphasize partnership rather than a traditional client-consultant relationship.
